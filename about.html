About Me

I am a mechanical engineer with a passion for robotics and aerospace

Community Involvement

During my time at Cal Poly Humboldt, I sought out opportunities to build community. As president of both the Aviation Club and the ASME chapter, I led community-building outreach and organizational efforts while helping foster a community of student leaders. In these roles, I helped oversee the installation of an Advanced Aviation Training Device (AATD), giving students and community members the opportunity to log instrument and visual flight hours.

Through the Aviation Club, we also organized workshops for students preparing to earn their FAA Part 107 Remote Pilot Certificate. Participating in these events further strengthened my interest in aviation, and my future goals include earning both my Part 107 certification and my private pilot's license.

Hobbies

Outside of engineering, I enjoy finding creative and challenging hobbies. I studied violin for 14 years before transitioning to the guitar, which I have now played for over a decade. I have also been playing board games since I was very young. For more than 20 years, I have played Dungeons & Dragons, becoming what is commonly known in the tabletop role-playing game (TTRPG) community as a "forever Dungeon Master." I have run games for groups ranging in size from three to eight players.

One hobby that has kept me engaged for years is Warhammer 40,000. What started as an interest in miniature painting quickly grew into a creative outlet that combines art, gaming, and craftsmanship. As my painting skills developed, I began documenting my progress on Instagram, and I've included some of my favorite pieces below.

Redemptor Dreadnought

This is my Redemptor Dreadnought and one of my best pieces. I completed this project over the course of three days, including both the model and its base. What I'm most proud of is the custom shoulder pauldron, which is not included in the original kit.

Space Marine

This model is part of the first collection of miniatures I painted after developing my current painting style. It was also my first attempt at creating a lens effect, visible in the miniature's eye piece. Mini dice are included for scale.
