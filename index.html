Robotic Inchworm Pruner (RIP-bot)

My capstone project was to design and evaluate a robotic system for pruning coastal redwood trees. The robotic system integrates climbing and cutting systems into an inchworm-style mechanism that improves worker safety in the field while reducing the cost per tree compared to using the same number of crew members for manual pruning.

Math Modeling Competition

I participated in the 2026 COMAP Mathematical Modeling Competition, where my team developed a model to predict the state of charge (SOC) of phone batteries while accounting for human behavior. My primary responsibility was designing the mathematical model's structure and underlying logic, which formed the foundation of our approach. To accomplish this, I applied fundamental linear algebra concepts developed through my computer science coursework and later refined in my Finite Element Analysis course.

2D Heat Conduction FEA

I developed a finite element analysis program to solve steady-state 2D heat conduction over a rectangular domain with prescribed temperature boundary conditions. The model assembled and solved the temperature field for multiple mesh densities, then compared the results against benchmark values along a verification line. This project strengthened my understanding of numerical modeling, mesh refinement, boundary conditions, and error analysis in finite element methods.
