Robotic Inchworm Pruner (RIP-bot)

My capstone project was to design and evaluate a robotic system for pruning Costal Redwood trees. The robotic system integrates the climbing and cutting systems into an inchworm style mechanism that imrpoves safety in the field for workers and reduces cost per tree of use for the same number of crew members that would otherwise prune the trees manually.

Math Modeling Competition

I participated in the 2026 COMAP Math Modeling Competition. My team and I developed a way to model state of charge (SOC) for phonebatteries taking into account human behavior. My primary role and contribution was developing the structure and logic for the mathmatical model. I used fundamental linear algebra concepts that I developed in my comp sci series and refined in my Finite Element Analysis Course.

2D Heat Conduction FEA

I developed a finite element analysis program to solve steady-state 2D heat conduction over a rectangular domain with prescribed temperature boundary conditions. The model assembled and solved the temperature field for multiple mesh densities, then compared the results against benchmark values along a verification line. This project strengthened my understanding of numerical modeling, mesh refinement, boundary conditions, and error analysis in finite element methods.
